## Try it on a literal mask

There are 2 different instances of a Nogaku (Noh theatre) mask called Doji. Things like the cosine distance metric of a face mesh can indeed tell that it's the same mask type, even if it's a different instance of the mask type. 

The mask is recognisable as a Doji mask if you're familiar with Noh theatre:

https://the-noh.com/sub/jp/index.php?mode=db&action=e_index&class_id=1

Specifically:

https://the-noh.com/sub/jp/index.php?mode=db&action=e_view_detail&data_id=30&class_id=1

There has been research on the ways Noh masks interact with light. They're carved from single pieces of wood using a 3D schematic of the final shape.

https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0071389

Example of mask carving process:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B7bPmVp4LRY
